New Capabilities of Image ➞ Image Translation

Based on: Unsupervised Image-to-Image Translation Networks

Ming-Yu Liu, Thomas Breuel, and Jan Kautz

Researchers at computer game company NVIDIA have been exploring new techniques of image-to-image translation.

This technique performs image translation, which means that the input is an image of our choice, and the output is the same image with somewhat different semantics.

For instance, a daytime photo or video can be converted to appear as if it were shot during the night, or an image in the winter can be translated to appear as if it were in the summer.

Approach

These translations are typically accomplished by using Generative Adversarial Networks.

This is a pair of neural networks where the generator network creates new synthetic images trying to fool a discriminator network which learns to tell a fake, synthesized image from a real one.

⇨

Approach

These 2 neural networks learn together, where one tries to come up with better solutions to fool the discriminator, where the discriminator seeks to get better at telling forgeries from the real photographs.

Winter ⇨ Summer

In the end, this rivalry makes both of them more accurate, and the final result is an excellent technique to create convincing image translations.

There was an earlier work that was able to perform image translation by learning on a novel cycle consistency constraint.

[Source: Zhu and Park et al. 2017]

This means that we assume that the source image can be translated to the target image and then this target image can be translated back to look exactly like the source.

⇨

Cycle-Consistent Networks

What this means is that these translations are not arbitrary and are mathematically meaningful operations.

[Source: Campbell et al. 2014]

Here, the new technique builds on a novel assumption that there exists a latent space in which the input and the output images can both coexist.

The theory of font design is complex.


But we can create a 2-dimensional latent space that encodes simple properties like curvature that can describe many fonts in an intuitive manner.

Applications


Remarkably, this new research also makes it possible to convert dogs and cats into different breeds!

Applications

Further, it an also perform real to synthetic image translation and vice versa.

[Source: Liu et al. 2017]

To Reality and Back

This means that it can create video game footage from our real world videos, and even more remarkably, convert video game footage to real world video!

Hold on to your papers because it can also perform attribute-based image translation!

This means that for instance, we can input an image of a human face and transform the model’s hair to blonde, add sunglasses or smiles to it at will.

Limitations

A limitation of this technique is that training is still non-trivial as it still relies on generative adversarial networks, and it is not yet clear whether there is a point to with the training converges or not.
