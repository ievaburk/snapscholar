4 Experiments Where AI Outsmarted its Creators

Based on: The Surprising Creativity of Digital Evolution: A Collection of Anecdotes from the Evolutionary Computation and Artificial Life Research Communities

Joel Lehman, Jeff Clune, Dusan Misevic, et al.

AI researchers have been baffled by the creativity and unexpected actions of some of their own creations...

①

In the first experiment, the robots were asked to walk around in a way that would minimize their foot contact with the ground.

To the researchers’ surprise, the robots showed that this can be done with 0% contact, meaning they never touch the ground with their feet…

The researchers were baffled at how this was possible, and pulled up a video for evidence. It showed the robot flipping over and walking using its elbows!

A different robot arm experiment also came to a surprising conclusion…

②

At first, the robot arm was tasked to grab a cube using its grippers, which it successfully learned to perform.

However, in a later experiment, the gripper was crippled, making the robot unable to open its fingers.

Researchers expected a pathetic video of the robot pushing the box around without picking up the cube. Instead, they found this. ⇨

Rather than using its fingers, the robot found the perfect angle to smash its hand against the cube to force the gripper to open and pick up the cube!

③

In the next experiment, a group of robots were tasked to find food, and avoid poisonous objects.


They were equipped with a light and no further instructions.

Deriving Community

They first learned to use the lights to communicate the presence of food and poisonous objects to each other.

This demonstrated that when trying to maximize the probability for survival of an entire colony, the concept of communication and cooperation can emerge even from simple neural networks.

Further, when a new reward system was created that fostered self-preservation, the robots learned to deceive each other by lighting up the food signal near the poisonous objects to take out their competitors and increase their chances for survival.

④

A different AI was asked to fix a faulty sorting computer program.

It soon achieved a perfect score without changing anything––it noticed that by short-circuiting the program itself, it can always provide an empty output.

Of course, if there are no numbers, there is nothing to sort––problem solved!

⇨

Hold on to your seat

With AI research improving at a rapid pace, we have seen it capabilities already surpass our expectations.

But we must make sure to formulate our problems with caution. The AI will try to use loopholes instead of common sense to solve the given issues.

In the case of a car chase with the robot AI wanting to minimize contact with the ground, you can expect to be promptly ejected from your car!
